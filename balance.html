What Is Balance?

Likely the first thing a mix engineer will do with a mix (and the easiest to explain), balancing put simply is the act of using the volume faders on your channels to get all your sounds sitting nicely with eachother. Used in conjuction with panning (moving your sounds left and right in the frequency spectrum) balancing can be used to give your song a rough mix before you get more surgical with EQ and compression.

How to use it.

Starting your leveling process can be done a few ways. Ideally, you will start with the loudest element in your mix (or the main element) and then raise your elements around it to compliment it. For example, you wouldn't want your guitars to be louder than your vocals and making them hard to hear or understand, for this circumstance you will start by bring your vocal to a level that sounds good to you and then bring in your other elements around it until they sound like they sit nicely without sticking out or being inaudible.
