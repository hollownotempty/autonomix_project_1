What Is EQ?

Ask any music producer what tool they use most, and nine-out-of-ten times they’re going to say EQ (short for equaliser). In simple terms, EQ is a tool that allows you to turn down the level of individual sections of frequencies (or ‘frequency bands’) in a signal. It is used to pull out harsh frequencies or push up ones we like, allowing us to change the sonic character of a sound graphically.

How to use it.

When it comes to setting your EQ up, there will be three settings to look at. These are your gain, cut-off and bandwidth (or Q).
Gain controls the amplitude of the frequency you are affecting, this can be a boost or a cut. Your cut-off is the point in the frequency spectrum at which a filter or EQ is applied. Your Q decides how broad your boost or cut is, with higher values being narrower towards your cut-off.

Your filter type will decide the type of equaliser you will be using, common filter types include:

  • Low cut - Cuts everything below the cut-off
  • High cut - Cuts everything above the cut-off
  • Bell - Boost or cut the chosen part of the frequency spectrum
  • High Shelf - Cut or boost all frequencies above the cut-off
  • Low Shelf - Cut or boost all frequencies below the cut-off
